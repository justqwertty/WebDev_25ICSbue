Molten Chocolate Lava Cakes

30 mins 7 ingredients 420 cal/serving

Individual chocolate cakes with a gooey, molten center. Serve warm with vanilla ice cream for the ultimate dessert experience.

Ingredients

6 oz high-quality dark chocolate
1/2 cup unsalted butter
2 whole eggs
2 egg yolks
1/4 cup granulated sugar
2 tbsp all-purpose flour
Pinch of salt
Powdered sugar for dusting

Instructions

1
Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C). Butter and flour 4 ramekins.
2
Melt chocolate and butter together in double boiler, then cool slightly.
3
Whisk eggs, yolks and sugar until pale and thick (about 5 mins).
4
Fold chocolate mixture into eggs, then fold in flour and salt.
5
Divide batter among ramekins. Bake 12-14 minutes until edges are set but center is soft.
6
Let sit 1 minute, then invert onto plates. Dust with powdered sugar.

Berry Swirl Cheesecake

6 hours 10 ingredients 380 cal/serving

Creamy no-bake cheesecake with a graham cracker crust and vibrant mixed berry swirl. Refreshing and perfect for summer.

Ingredients

2 cups graham cracker crumbs
1/2 cup melted butter
2 tbsp sugar
16 oz cream cheese, softened
1 cup powdered sugar
2 tsp vanilla extract
1 cup heavy cream, whipped
1 cup mixed berries
2 tbsp lemon juice
1 tbsp cornstarch

Instructions

1
Mix crumbs, melted butter and sugar. Press into 9" springform pan. Chill 30 mins.
2
Beat cream cheese until smooth. Add powdered sugar and vanilla.
3
Fold in whipped cream. Spread over crust.
4
Purée berries with lemon juice. Strain, then cook with cornstarch until thickened.
5
Drizzle berry sauce over cheesecake and swirl with knife.
6
Chill at least 4 hours, preferably overnight.

Authentic Italian Tiramisu

45 mins 9 ingredients 350 cal/serving

Layers of coffee-soaked ladyfingers and mascarpone cream, dusted with cocoa powder. The ultimate no-bake Italian dessert.

Ingredients

6 egg yolks
3/4 cup granulated sugar
16 oz mascarpone cheese
1 1/2 cups heavy cream
2 cups strong espresso, cooled
3 tbsp coffee liqueur (optional)
24 ladyfinger cookies
1/4 cup cocoa powder
Dark chocolate shavings

Instructions

1
Whisk yolks and sugar over double boiler until pale and thick (about 10 mins).
2
Remove from heat, beat in mascarpone until smooth.
3
Whip cream to stiff peaks, then fold into mascarpone mixture.
4
Mix espresso with liqueur. Quickly dip ladyfingers (1-2 seconds per side).
5
Layer dipped ladyfingers, then cream mixture in 9x13" dish. Repeat layers.
6
Chill 6 hours or overnight. Dust with cocoa and chocolate shavings before serving.

Classic Crème Brûlée

4 hours 6 ingredients 450 cal/serving

Silky vanilla custard with a crisp caramelized sugar topping. The perfect balance of creamy and crunchy textures.

Ingredients

2 cups heavy cream
1 vanilla bean (or 1 tsp extract)
5 egg yolks
1/2 cup granulated sugar
Pinch of salt
3 tbsp turbinado sugar (for topping)

Instructions

1
Preheat oven to 325°F (160°C). Heat cream and vanilla (scraped bean) until just simmering.
2
Whisk yolks, sugar and salt until pale. Slowly whisk in warm cream.
3
Strain mixture into 4 ramekins. Place in baking dish, add hot water halfway up sides.
4
Bake 30-35 minutes until set but still jiggly in center. Cool, then chill 4 hours.
5
Sprinkle tops with turbinado sugar. Caramelize with torch or broiler until golden.
6
Let sit 5 minutes before serving to allow sugar to harden.

Perfect Apple Pie

2 hours 12 ingredients 380 cal/serving

Flaky buttery crust filled with cinnamon-spiced apples. The quintessential American dessert with a perfect crust-to-filling ratio.

Ingredients

2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
1 tsp salt
1 cup unsalted butter, chilled
6-8 tbsp ice water
6-7 medium apples, peeled and sliced
3/4 cup brown sugar
2 tbsp lemon juice
2 tsp cinnamon
1/4 tsp nutmeg
1/4 tsp allspice
2 tbsp butter, diced
1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)

Instructions

1
Mix flour and salt. Cut in butter until pea-sized. Add water until dough forms.
2
Divide dough in half, chill 1 hour. Roll out bottom crust, place in pie dish.
3
Toss apples with sugar, lemon juice and spices. Let sit 30 minutes.
4
Fill crust with apples, dot with butter. Cover with top crust, seal edges.
5
Cut vents, brush with egg wash. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 50-60 minutes.
6
Cool 2 hours before slicing to allow filling to set.

Vanilla Bean Panna Cotta

6 hours 7 ingredients 320 cal/serving

Silky Italian custard with real vanilla bean, served with fresh berry coulis. Elegant yet simple to prepare.

Ingredients

2 cups heavy cream
1/2 cup whole milk
1/2 cup sugar
1 vanilla bean, split
2 tsp gelatin
2 tbsp cold water
Pinch of salt
1 cup mixed berries (for coulis)

Instructions

1
Sprinkle gelatin over cold water, let bloom 5 minutes.
2
Heat cream, milk, sugar and vanilla (scraped seeds and pod) until steaming.
3
Remove from heat, stir in gelatin until dissolved. Strain.
4
Pour into 6 ramekins, chill 4+ hours until set.
5
Purée berries with 1 tbsp sugar for coulis.
6
To serve, dip ramekins in hot water for 10 seconds, invert onto plates. Drizzle with coulis.

Dark Chocolate Mousse

4 hours 6 ingredients 350 cal/serving

Light yet rich chocolate mousse made with just a few quality ingredients. The perfect make-ahead dessert for entertaining.

Ingredients

7 oz high-quality dark chocolate
3 tbsp unsalted butter
4 eggs, separated
1/4 cup granulated sugar
Pinch of salt
1/2 tsp vanilla extract
Whipped cream for garnish

Instructions

1
Melt chocolate and butter together, let cool slightly.
2
Whisk egg yolks with half the sugar until pale and thick.
3
Fold chocolate mixture into yolks. Add vanilla.
4
Beat egg whites with salt until foamy. Gradually add remaining sugar, beat to stiff peaks.
5
Gently fold whites into chocolate mixture in three additions.
6
Divide among glasses, chill 4+ hours. Serve with whipped cream.

French Lemon Tart

2 hours 10 ingredients 320 cal/serving

Buttery shortcrust pastry filled with tangy lemon curd. The perfect balance of sweet and tart flavors.

Ingredients

1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
1/2 cup powdered sugar
1/2 cup cold butter, cubed
1 egg yolk
4 eggs
3/4 cup granulated sugar
1/2 cup lemon juice (about 3 lemons)
1 tbsp lemon zest
1/2 cup heavy cream
Pinch of salt

Instructions

1
Pulse flour, powdered sugar and butter in food processor until crumbly.
2
Add yolk, pulse until dough forms. Press into tart pan, chill 30 mins.
3
Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Blind bake crust 15 mins, remove weights, bake 5 more mins.
4
Whisk eggs, sugar, lemon juice and zest. Stir in cream.
5
Pour filling into warm crust. Bake 25-30 mins until set but slightly wobbly.
6
Cool completely. Dust with powdered sugar before serving.

Caramel Bread Pudding

1 hour 15 mins 9 ingredients 420 cal/serving

Rich custard-soaked brioche with swirls of caramel sauce. Served warm with vanilla ice cream for the ultimate comfort dessert.

Ingredients

8 cups brioche or challah, cubed
4 eggs
2 cups whole milk
1 cup heavy cream
3/4 cup brown sugar
1 tsp vanilla extract
1/2 tsp cinnamon
1/4 tsp nutmeg
1/2 cup caramel sauce

Instructions

1
Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Butter a 9x13" baking dish.
2
Spread bread cubes in dish. Whisk eggs, milk, cream, sugar and spices.
3
Pour custard over bread, pressing down to soak. Drizzle with half the caramel.
4
Let sit 30 minutes for bread to absorb liquid.
5
Bake 45-50 minutes until puffed and golden. Let cool 15 minutes.
6
Serve warm with remaining caramel sauce and ice cream.
